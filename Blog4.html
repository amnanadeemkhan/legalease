Criminal Law

Criminal law is to control the influence of the state in all matters.

Criminal law in Pakistan is primarily governed by the Pakistan Penal Code, which was enacted in 1860 during British rule and has since been amended to reflect contemporary legal norms and societal needs. The Penal Code defines various criminal offenses and prescribes punishments for those found guilty. Additionally, other laws, such as the Code of Criminal Procedure and the Anti-Terrorism Act, provide procedural guidelines and address specific types of criminal activity, including terrorism, cybercrime, and corruption. The criminal justice system in Pakistan operates through a hierarchical structure of courts, including magisterial courts, sessions courts, and the High Courts, with the Supreme Court serving as the highest appellate authority. Investigations into criminal offenses are conducted by law enforcement agencies, such as the police, who gather evidence and submit it to the relevant court for adjudication. Trials are conducted according to established legal procedures, with the accused afforded certain rights, including the right to legal representation and the presumption of innocence until proven guilty. Sentences for criminal convictions may include imprisonment, fines, or other forms of punishment deemed appropriate under the law. Despite the existence of legal frameworks, challenges such as delays in the justice system, corruption, and inadequate enforcement of laws persist, necessitating ongoing efforts to strengthen the rule of law and protect the rights of individuals within the criminal justice system in Pakistan.
